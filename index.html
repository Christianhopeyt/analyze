Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about YouTube revenue estimation

We use RPM (Revenue Per Mille) data based on the channel's detected niche, geographic audience, content type, and engagement metrics. These figures are derived from industry-standard benchmarks used by advertising platforms globally.

Channel statistics (subscribers, views, videos) are retrieved from the YouTube Data API v3, which is the official public data source. Revenue estimates are our proprietary calculations — they are not provided directly by YouTube.

Our estimates fall within a plausible range based on real market RPM data. Actual earnings vary based on advertiser demand, seasonal trends, individual channel contracts, and the portion of views that are monetized. We provide minimum, average, and maximum ranges to reflect this variability.

RPM (Revenue Per Mille) is the amount a creator earns per 1,000 video views. It varies widely — finance and business channels can earn $10–25 RPM, while gaming or entertainment channels typically see $2–5 RPM. Geography also plays a major role: US and UK audiences carry the highest RPM globally.

Focus on higher-RPM niches, target English-speaking audiences, create longer videos to increase ad placements, publish consistently, and diversify revenue streams through sponsorships, memberships, and affiliate marketing. Growing subscribers also improves your negotiating power for brand deals.
