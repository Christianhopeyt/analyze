YouTube Partner Program Requirements in 2026: Everything You Need to Qualify

The exact YouTube Partner Program requirements in 2026 — subscriber thresholds, watch hours, Shorts criteria, and policy requirements explained clearly.

Getting into the YouTube Partner Program (YPP) is the first concrete milestone for any creator serious about generating income from their channel. The requirements have remained stable since the 2023 update, and this guide explains the current 2026 thresholds precisely — including the separate criteria for channels focused on long-form video versus Shorts.

The Two YPP Tiers in 2026

YouTube currently operates two distinct YPP entry tiers, introduced progressively since 2023. The tier you enter determines what monetization features you unlock at the point of approval:

Key point for 2026: If you create primarily long-form content, the path to full monetization requires 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 valid watch hours in the last 12 months. For Shorts-focused creators, the alternative path requires 1,000 subscribers and 10 million valid Shorts views in 90 days. Both paths lead to the same full ad monetization tier.

What "Valid" Watch Hours and Views Actually Mean

YouTube applies filters to watch hours and Shorts views before counting them toward YPP thresholds. Watch hours from unlisted or private videos do not count. Shorts views from videos that violate community guidelines are excluded. Any artificially inflated views from bots or click farms result in disqualification and potentially permanent channel bans.

YouTube audits applications manually, so a channel that just crossed the threshold with borderline content should expect a more thorough policy review before approval.

Policy Requirements Alongside the Numbers

Meeting the numeric thresholds is necessary but not sufficient. Your channel must also satisfy all of the following at the time of application:

  • No active Community Guidelines strikes
  • No active Copyright strikes
  • Two-factor authentication enabled on the Google Account
  • AdSense account in good standing (or willingness to create one)
  • Channel based in a country where the YouTube Partner Program is available
  • Content is original and adheres to YouTube's advertiser-friendly content guidelines

How Long Does YPP Approval Take in 2026?

YouTube's current review timeline is typically 7–30 days from application submission. Channels with a clear, consistent content focus and no policy flags are typically approved at the shorter end. Channels with mixed content, prior strikes that were resolved, or content in borderline categories (news, commentary, gaming with mature themes) typically take longer.

If your application is rejected, YouTube provides a reason and allows reapplication after 30 days.

What to Expect Immediately After Approval

Most creators are surprised by how modest AdSense revenue is in the first months after monetization. At 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 watch hours, a channel is typically generating between $10 and $60 per month in AdSense revenue, depending on niche and audience geography. This is a starting point, not a destination.
